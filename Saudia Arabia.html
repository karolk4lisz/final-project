National Dish of Saudi Arabia



Jareesh

Jareesh is a traditional Saudi Arabian dish made from crushed wheat and meat, typically cooked in a clay pot. Saudi culinary Arts commission declared that Jareesh should be Saudi Arabia's National dish, since reference to the dish has been made over centuries.

Traditional Recipe for Jareesh

​

Ingredients

1 cup jareesh (crushed wheat)

500g lamb or chicken, cut into small
pieces

1 large onion, finely chopped

3 cloves of garlic, minced

2 tablespoons vegetable oil

2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon ground coriander

1 teaspoon ground black pepper

Salt to taste

Water for cooking

Optional toppings:
fried onions, chopped fresh parsley
or cilantro

Method

1. Rinse the jareesh thoroughly under cold water and soak it in water for at least 1 hour. Drain and set aside.

2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until golden brown and fragrant.

3. Add the meat pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides. This helps to develop the flavors.

4. Sprinkle the ground cumin, coriander, black pepper, and salt over the meat. Stir well to coat the meat evenly with the spices.

5. Pour enough water into the pot to cover the meat completely. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1 hour or until the meat becomes tender.

6. Once the meat is tender, add the soaked jareesh to the pot. Stir well to combine the jareesh with the meat and spices.

7. Continue cooking the jareesh on low heat, stirring occasionally, for about 1-2 hours or until the jareesh grains are soft and well-cooked. Add more water if needed to maintain a thick, porridge-like consistency.

8. In a separate small pan, melt the ghee over medium heat. Add the ghee to the jareesh and stir it in to enhance the flavor.

9. Continue cooking the jareesh for another 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper according to your taste.

10. Serve the jareesh hot in individual bowls. You can garnish each serving with fried onions and chopped fresh parsley or cilantro for added flavor and presentation.
